Free practical guide

How to build a professional Google Form from a blank page.

A clean step-by-step guide for creating a registration form, organizing questions into sections, configuring settings, linking responses to Sheets, testing the form and sharing it confidently.

Example form

RCCG The King's Palace AI Bootcamp 2026 Registration Form.

This guide uses a training registration form as the example, but the same structure works for schools, churches, businesses, NGOs, workshops, bootcamps and event registrations.

Build sequence

From blank page to published form.

Create the form

Open Google Forms, choose a blank form, then give it a clear title. Example: RCCG The King's Palace AI Bootcamp 2026 Registration Form.

Add a clear description

Explain what the programme is, who it is for, the date, time, venue, registration requirement and enquiry phone number. Keep this section factual and easy to scan.

Upload a header image

Add a branded header image if available. Preview on desktop and mobile because Google Forms may crop the image differently across screens.

Create participant information fields

Add fields for full name, gender, age range, phone number, email address, church/parish or organization, and emergency contact where appropriate.

Add participant category

Use multiple choice or dropdown fields for categories such as teenager, student, parent, church worker, professional, business owner, minister or invited guest.

Collect education and work information

Ask for school, occupation, class level, department or role only where the information will help planning, grouping or follow-up.

Check digital access and experience

Ask whether the participant has a smartphone, laptop, internet access, Gmail account and previous AI experience. This helps facilitators plan support.

Ask for expectations

Include one or two open-ended questions asking what participants hope to learn and what problem they want AI to help them solve.

Add parent or guardian information

If minors are attending, add guardian name, phone number and consent fields. Make these required for underage participants.

Add consent and commitment

Use checkboxes for attendance commitment, respectful conduct, data accuracy and permission to contact the participant about the programme.

Configure form settings

Review whether to collect email addresses, limit to one response, allow response editing, show progress bar and shuffle questions. For registration forms, do not shuffle questions.

Write the confirmation message

Thank the participant, confirm that registration has been received, restate the programme date/time and give the enquiry contact.

Link responses to Google Sheets

Open the Responses tab and create a linked spreadsheet. Use it for attendance lists, communication, grouping and post-training reporting.

Test before publishing

Submit a test response from desktop and mobile. Confirm required questions, branching, confirmation message and spreadsheet capture all work correctly.

Share the form

Copy the form link, shorten it if needed, and share through WhatsApp, email, church groups, school groups, posters, QR codes and official announcements.

Question checklist

Recommended sections for a training registration form.

Use these sections when building a serious training or bootcamp form. They make the form cleaner and make the response spreadsheet easier to use.

  • Participant information
  • Participant category
  • Education and work information
  • Digital access and AI experience
  • Training expectations
  • Parent or guardian information, when needed
  • Consent and commitment
Required

Name, phone and category

These fields help identify and contact each participant.

Useful

Device and internet access

This helps facilitators know who may need extra practical support.

Review

Test response before launch

Never publish a public form before submitting at least one test entry.
